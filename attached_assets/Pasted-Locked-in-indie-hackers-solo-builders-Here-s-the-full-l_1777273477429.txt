Locked in: indie hackers / solo builders. Here's the full landscape — every meaningful problem they have and the feature ideas that solve it. I've grouped them into pillars so you can see how they fit into a true hub.

1. Identity & Profile (who you are as a builder)
The profile sets the tone for everything else. It's not a CV — it's "what I'm building right now."

Builder card — name, current project, stack, niche, stage (idea / building / launched / making $)
"Looking for" tags — feedback, first users, co-founder, designer, accountability buddy
"I can help with" tags — SEO, design, copywriting, code reviews, intros
Live status — coding now / on a break / shipping today / heads down
Auto-pulled stats (optional, opt-in) — MRR from Stripe, GitHub commits, weekly traffic
Builder reputation — earned from helpful feedback, mentoring, intros given
2. The "We're All Here Together" Layer (the closeness magic)
This is the heart of your "feels close" vision.

Home rooms — small persistent spaces (50–300 people) by niche: SaaS, AI tools, mobile, ecommerce, devtools, etc. Same people every day = real recognition.
Live presence dot — see who's online, who's coding, who's in a focus session right now
Coworking rooms — silent or chatty drop-in rooms with a shared timer (like Focusmate but for the whole community)
Daily "what are you shipping today?" thread — one-tap morning check-in
Founder pods — small fixed groups of 5 builders at similar stages, weekly check-ins
Casual lounge — one always-on chat for off-topic, jokes, vent moments
Voice/video drop-ins — like Discord stages but lightweight; a builder hits "I'm in" and others can join
3. Build-in-Public Layer
Make sharing progress effortless and rewarding.

Project pages — every member's project gets a mini landing page inside the app
Build log — append-only timeline of updates, like a journal anyone can follow
Milestone celebrations — first user, first $, first 100 users, first $1K MRR auto-trigger a community celebration
Weekly recap auto-post — "This week I shipped X, learned Y, struggled with Z"
Public roadmap with upvotes from your followers
Streaks — ship streak, post streak, code streak (gentle, not toxic)
4. Feedback & Validation
The #1 thing indie hackers chase. Make it structured and high-signal.

Idea critique threads — post your idea in a structured form, get scored feedback (clarity, market, differentiation)
Product showcase — drop your product for honest reviews from other builders
Landing page roast — pin a screenshot, get UX/copy advice
Beta tester exchange — "I'll test yours if you test mine" board
Quick polls & surveys — built-in tool, no SurveyMonkey needed
Pricing review — show your pricing page, get suggestions
5. Distribution & First Users
Solving the hardest part of indie hacking: getting the first 100 users.

Launch board — schedule and announce launches; followers get notified
"Launching today" feed — community rallies around live launches
First-100 Club — focused space for builders trying to get their first traction
Cross-promo swap board — newsletter swaps, X shoutout swaps, audience trades
Member product directory — browseable like a community-curated Product Hunt
Sponsorship matching — find sponsors for your newsletter/project
6. Accountability & Momentum
Solo builders drift. Fix that with social pressure (the good kind).

Daily ship calendar — green-square chart of days you shipped something
Friday updates ritual — one win, one struggle, one ask, posted to your pod
4-week sprint cohorts — start a goal with 5 others, finish together
Public goal commitments with deadlines and reactions
Coworking timer — synchronized 50-min focus blocks with others online
7. Learning & Mentorship
The "I wish I knew someone who's done this" problem.

Office hours — experienced founders open 15-min slots, book in one tap
Playbook library — short, searchable guides ("How I got my first 100 users", "How I picked pricing")
AMA series — monthly Ask-Me-Anything threads with successful indie hackers
Skill swaps — "I'll teach you SEO, teach me design"
Resource library — vetted tools, templates, frameworks
Failure stories — postmortems from shut-down projects (high learning value)
8. Collaboration & Hiring
When you need hands but not a full hire.

Co-founder matching — based on stack, niche, working style, location
Micro-gigs board — 1-day to 1-week paid help (designer for a logo, dev for a bug)
Help-wanted listings — short-term collaboration requests
Skills marketplace — list what you can offer for hire or barter
9. Money & Growth
The revenue and fundraising side, indie-hacker style.

Revenue rooms — private spaces by MRR tier ($0–1K, $1–10K, $10K+) so you talk to peers, not aliens
Grants & no-equity funding tracker — curated list with deadlines (Microns, Shuttleworth, etc.)
Indie-friendly accelerator list
Pricing review threads — already mentioned but worth repeating, this is gold
10. Mental Health & Real Talk
Often missing from communities and where you can stand out.

"Real talk" threads — vent, doubt, failure, normalize the messy middle
Burnout self-check — weekly mood/energy pulse, gentle nudges to rest
Anonymous support posts — for stuff people won't put their name on
Celebration broadcasts — community-wide shout when someone hits a milestone
Builder stories — long-form interviews of regular members, not just famous ones
11. Live Moments & Events
Make the app feel alive with rituals everyone shows up for.

Demo nights — monthly live demos, voted on by the community
Launch parties — when a member launches, others hop on a live call to support
Co-founder speed-dating events — recurring matchmaking sessions
Weekend build challenges / hackathons — themed, lightweight, fun
Quarterly community retreats (virtual or IRL meetups)
12. Sticky "Useful Things" (the hub extras)
The non-social tools that make people open the app daily, not just for community.

Idea journal — built-in scratchpad for half-baked ideas
Bookmark library — save articles, tweets, products you want to revisit
Indie newsletter feed — read top indie hacker newsletters in one place
Event & conference calendar — indie-friendly events worldwide
Domain name brainstormer
Business setup checklists — by country (LLC, taxes, payments)
AI co-pilot — "review my landing copy", "score my pricing", "summarize feedback I got this week"
AI matchmaker — "find me 3 builders most similar to me right now"
Browser extension — share what you're working on with one click from any tab
My recommended MVP — the smallest version that still feels like a hub
Trying to ship all 60+ features = death. Here's the leanest set that already feels like a true hub:

The MVP (5 things):

Builder profile with current project + "looking for" / "can help with" tags
Home rooms by niche (start with 4–5 rooms, not 50)
Daily presence + "what are you shipping today" thread — the heartbeat
Project pages + build log — sharing progress effortlessly
Feedback exchange — one structured thread type for idea/product/landing-page critique
